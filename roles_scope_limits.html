The BBG Project - Roles, Scope & Limits

Roles

Adam Ballantine – Head of R&D

Adam is the initiator of the BBG project. He has done the majority of building, coding, and testing of the Baby Boom Gate device. His vision to find a solution to a problem millions of parents face is the driving force for the BBG project. He has the gate in question to be closed in possession. If the device was anywhere else, it could not be tested on the original gate for which the project was designed.

Alexander Goldberg – Chief Marketing Officer

Alexander has years of experience in a creative capacity and has been engaged in the creation of the presentation video, together with Ashley. He managed this project from storyboard to script to recording. He has also engaged in the design and market research for the product. Both Alexander and Ashley have extensive experience in this field.

Ashley Wilkie – Editor and Content Marketer

Ashley has worked with Alexander on the presentation video, focusing on creating content for the presentation and editing the sections of the video together. Both Alexander and Ashley have extensive experience in this field.

Jessica Warburton – Office Manager

Jessica has taken on the role of piecing together the sections of the Assignment and filling in gaps from the material created for A2. She has ensured that everybody is contributing to their required space and keeping the project on track for completion. Her organisational skills have been invaluable for this project.

Karsten Beck – Chief IT Officer

Karsten has taken the content created for the assignment and translated it into an amazing website for the BBG (Baby Boom Gate) company. Karsten has extensive experience in this role and did an incredible job with it for A2, so has continued in the same vein.

Paul Archie – Chief Financial Officer

Paul has researched individual components for the Baby Boom Gate device, including where to purchase and maintaining an overall budget for the project. His efforts have ensured that Adam was able to find what he needed to build the device without having to purchase endless supplies to keep experimenting.
